Common Procedures IV A very tiny plastic tube is placed in the vein to allow medications and fluid to be injected directly into the blood stream. IV’s are usually quick to insert and have a low risk of infection. However, they usually do not last for more than a couple of days. Central Venous Line A larger version of an “IV”, this is a longer tube that sits in larger vessels of the body. This type of IV is used for:
  • Children who have very small blood vessels that make regular IV placement difficult.
  • Children who need medicine that is too strong for regular IV’s.
  • Children who are likely to need IV’s for multiple days in a row.
 Arterial Line This is similar to an IV, but instead of being placed in a vein, this type of tiny plastic tube is placed in the artery. This type of line is excellent for measuring minute-to- minute changes in a child’s blood pressure. It also allows us to accurately check a child’s blood oxygen level.
